5.0 out of 5 starsThe Heavy Metal compromise solution is here!
ByEric Stevenson November 28, 2012
How do you get that Heavy Metal- Hard Rock Crunch that we all seek without having the all day grip strength of the Incredible Hulk & without playing heavy strings all day? Well here is your solution......Strings heavy enough to push even the most stubborn pickups into clean overdrive.

I play all my Heavy metal/Rock on a Japanese made Westone Spectrum H/S/H guitar fitted with custom pickups and have always had to compromise between playabilty and overdriving sustain of heavier strings. My end solution has been D'addario EXL115 strings down tuned half-a-step to flat which allows the ability to slacken the strings, easier bending of heavier strings, and allows the string to vibrate and sustain with a deeper growl.

Now nothing is ever perfect when it comes to guitar distortion but I believe there is a very good solution for most of your problems to be found with these Great Quality strings at bargain 'don't break the bank' price.

Go ahead try'em but don't forget that you will always have to reintonate that Floyd Rose style bridge quite a bit- so have your best Heavy metal/Rock Guitar set especially aside for these lil' beauties then you will always be at the ready when the Rocker in you is ready to roll.

5.0 out of 5 starsPerfect hybrid string set
ByJohn C. Harmon Jr.on July 31, 2013
Format: Electronics|Verified Purchase
I used to use the D'Addario EXL116 (11-52) on all of my Eb Strats. For me the wound strings, especially the low E, were too chunky and overbearing, both in feel and sound. I went to these (11-49) and couldn't be happier! Love the tone, love the feel! I float the trem on my Strats and the tuning stability is spot on.

I had a similar experience with the Ernie Ball equivalent, but I've been using D'Addarios for years and with the "Subscribe and Save" program, you just can't beat it!

BTW, I still use D'Addario EXL110 (10-46) on my standard-tuned Strats because, well, D'Addario Rocks!.
