5.0 out of 5 starsNew colors for 2012 release
ByJayhanon October 4, 2012
All that I can find with the new release is the updated colors, the rest looks the same. This is still 4th generation Apple iPod Shuffle (from wikipedia, this new color refresh is still under 4th generation category).

This product is small, actually very small. I know you can use your iPhones or Androids and even get more storage space than this, but shuffle has a different target of users, mostly for active and on the go users. This is perfect for exercising, running, walking, etc. Just clip it to your shirt and your ready to go.

If your the type of user who wants to put all your mp3s to your mp3 player, then you might want to find a different player thas has larger capacity , as this one has only 2gb of space. You should still be able to put hundreds of songs here especially if your songs bitrate compression is not very high.

Some of the basic functionalities:

Shuffle switch
Left - shuffle music
Middle - play in order
Right - turn off

VoiceOver button
Single click - hear current track and artist
Single click and hold - hear playlist menu.
Double click - hear battery status

PROS:
- Tiny (this can also be a con, in a way like since it's tiny, it's also easy to lose or misplace)
- 15hr play time
- Colors refreshed for the 2012 release model
- Physical buttons
- High quality anodized aluminum

CONS:
- Expensive (In my opinion, $49 for 2gb is expensive)
- No option for a higher storage capacity

What you get in the box:
- iPod shuffle
- Apple Earphones
- iPod shuffle USB Cable
- Quick Start guide

I'm highly recommending iPod shuffle. But do your research first if you want this kind of mp3 player..

4.0 out of 5 starsGreat little gadget
ByBlue Dragonflyon January 11, 2014
Color: Purple|Verified Purchase
I really wanted an iPod, but could not afford the iPod Touch or nano. So, I went for this one. It has really good sound and suits my needs. I download audiobooks from my local "Library 2 Go" and this is very handy for me to listen to audiobooks when I need something mobile. The only drawback is that I can't really download more than one item at a time to it, because they are too hard to find without a menu screen. As a basic "on the go" player it is perfect and I love the little clip. It does bookmark where I stopped in the audiobook, which is handy. The 2GB can hold approximately 2 large audiobooks, but as I mentioned, I only use it for one at a time. I did test how much of my music it would hold, just out of curiosity. My whole music library fit on it with room to spare. That is 66 songs at 751.5 MB, which is probably a lot less than most people have. The sound was clear and of good quality. The earbuds didn't fit very well, but I bought some silicone caps for them (from Amazon) that come in different sizes and this solved the problem. One of these days I'd like an iPod Touch, but for now, this works.

1.0 out of 5 starsDied Immediately After Warranty Expired
ByJ. W. Morganon August 18, 2013
Verified Purchase
This product made me wish that there was a middle ground between it and the Nano. The Nano has far too much functionality for my needs, so I did not want to spend three times as much. The big downside is that setting up your playlists for the shuffle is incredibly tedious.

On the plus side, I really like how light weight it is. I wear it on my collar when jogging and don't even feel it. Furthermore, although part of it is touching my skin the entire time, there is no chafing. Finally, its controls are much better than the Nano. It is easy to change the volume or switch songs.

9/23/2013 UPDATE: Reduced rating to one star

My Shuffle died three days after the one-year warranty expired. When I contacted Apple, they never responded. So, I essentially paid $1 per week to use it. I am hesitant to buy a replacement because I do not have faith in the product's longevity. And, therefore, can no longer recommend this product. I never used it in the rain, exposed it to the elements, dropped it, or did anything else that might shorten an electronic product's useful life.

4.0 out of 5 starsStrong clip, weaker battery than before.
ByDaveon May 5, 2013
Color: Slate|Verified Purchase
I use this at work in a busy warehouse. I've been using one of the same generation, but an earlier model for a while now. Somehow I lost that one and ordered a new one since it was so cheap. It is indeed a genuine apple product. The clip on this generation is very strong. Mine gets scraped and somewhat thrown around because the clip keeps it on my hip to get scraped, and doesn't let it fall off. But I'd rather it be scratched than it not be there at all. Headphone jack holds on to your plug very well. I don't think I've seen a stronger hold. It's somewhat of a second clip. Keeps it attached to you even it it gets scraped off. Buttons still have a strong clicking feel when pressed. Shows no sign of wearing out. My only complaint is the battery life. I am using the same headphones as with the one of a previous model, but the same generation, the same volume level, and filled to the brim with music, and it seems like the battery life isn't as good as my last one. It's only an hour to an hour and a half difference, but that can make or break a product. I don't mind it so much I guess. It causes me to take the very portable charging cord along with me to work. And with the USB cord, you can use it as an emergency flash drive. Has saved my butt a couple of times. Overall a wonderful iPod if your on the move, and your not too picky about the next song you hear.
